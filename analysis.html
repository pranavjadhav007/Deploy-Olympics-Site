India first participated in the Olympic Games in 1900. India has emerged as a competitive country in the past few years. It has dominated the hockey game at the Olympics with 12 Medals. India is ranked 51st on the overall list of Olympic medal winners. According to the Indian Olympic Association, India has won a total of 35 Olympic medals, including 10 gold, 9 silver, and 16 bronze.

As of the 2020 Games, all of the current 206 NOCs have participated in at least two editions of the Olympic Games. The Games have been held in nineteen countries over five continents: four times in the United States (1904, 1932, 1984, and 1996); three times in Great Britain (1908, 1948, and 2012); twice each in Greece (1896 and 2004), France (1900 and 1924), Germany (1936 and 1972), Australia (1956 and 2000), and Japan (1964 and 2020); and once each in Sweden (1912), Belgium (1920), the Netherlands (1928), Finland (1952), Italy (1960), Mexico (1968), Canada (1976), the Soviet Union (1980), South Korea (1988), Spain (1992), China (2008), and Brazil (2016).

Over time, the number of female athletes slowly increased, although it did not hit the ten percent mark until 1952, or the quarter mark until 1988; while the highest level of female participation was in 2020, where almost 5,400 women competed (49 percent). In the 2020 Olympics in Tokyo, a number of mixed shooting events, table tennis doubles, and swimming and sprinting relays were introduced in an attempt to improve participation among female athletes

Between 1992 and 2021, the average ages of male Olympians increased from 25 to 27 and those of female Olympians increased from 24 to 26. We found that older athletes are not just participating; they’re winning medals.

Males have dominated the Olympics participation. Here are some games where the difference in participation between females and males is less. New games  were introduced to increase female participation. Over the years, females have shown more participation.
